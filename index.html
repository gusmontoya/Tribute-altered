Louis Pasteur

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a time line of Louis Pasteur's life

  • 27th december, 1822

    Born in France

    Born in Dole, Jura, France, to a Catholic family of a poor tanner.
    He was the third child of Jean-Joseph Pasteur and Jeanne-Etiennette Roqui.

  • 1847

    Graduated from Ecole Normal University, France

    Submitted his two thesis in Chemistry and Physics.

  • 1854

    Dean of the new faculty of sciences at University of Lille, France

    Began his studies in fermentation. Where he is known to have coined
    his famous qoute "In the field of observation, chance favors only the prepared mind"

  • 1864

    The Germ Theory

    Many people in the 1800's still believed that germs were not harmful to people.
    Louis proved that germs were the cause of illness.

  • 1879

    Discovers Vaccinations

    Louis Pasteur discovered a vaccination for Chicken cholera
    which led to other vaccinaions like , Anthrax (1870s)
    Swine erysipelas (1882), and Rabies (1885).

  • 28th september, 1895

    Louis Pasteur dies

    Pasteur's health deteriorates after his first paralysis which occured before his
    70th birthday. Soon after his 70th birthday his paralysis worsens and dies. He is buried
    in the cathedral of Notre-Dame de Paris.
